Why it matters for E. coli

Cells do not store every wiggle of the world. They look for repeating structure — nutrients that pulse, gradients that cycle, rhythms imposed by day and environment.

A frequency is a shorthand for “this pattern keeps coming back.” Sensing in the frequency domain helps filter random noise and supports decisions: move, consume, or reprogram metabolism.

Key insight

  • Time domain — what happens, moment by moment.
  • Frequency domain — what repeats, and how strongly.
  • FFT — the bridge between those two views.
